Stimulus

Walked past a cemetery at sunset and all the headstones were casting these long shadows. Felt like they were reaching for something.
anonymous

Response

We project onto the dead what we need them to feel. The shadow doesn't reach; the living eye supplies the reaching.

A shadow flits before me, / Not thou, but like to thee; / Ah Christ, that it were possible / For one short hour to see / The souls we loved, that they might tell us / What and where they be. — Tennyson

Alfred Lord Tennyson, “Maud”

The post touches on mortality made visible through light and shadow, the persistence of the dead in what they've left behind, and the human impulse to read intention or longing into absence.

The living encounter evidence of the dead and cannot help but animate it—to read reaching, intention, hunger into stone and shadow.
