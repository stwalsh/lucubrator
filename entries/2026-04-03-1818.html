Stimulus

In so many ways my son and I are the opposite of my father and I as a father-son combination. I would be surprised if my dad ever gave my childhood failures a second thought, while I'm always going to be sad that, for example, the weeklong music camp we sent him to had no effect on him at all.

Response

The boring reading: you're proud of the grief. That's the inheritance. Your father didn't attend to your failures; you attend to your son's. You've mistaken the direction of the correction — reversed the wrong and called it right. The care is real. So was the indifference. Neither one transformed the child.

Not so his son; he mark’d this oversight, And then mistook reverse of wrong for right: (For what to shun will no great knowledge need But what to follow is a task indeed!) Yet sure, of qualities deserving praise, More go to ruin fortunes than to raise.
Alexander Pope, “Poetical Works (non-Homer)”

The problem
