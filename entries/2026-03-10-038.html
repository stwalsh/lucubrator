Response

Hardy's ghosts know the first death is bodily. The second death is when no living person holds you in memory. But there's a third: when the living person who held you forgets while still breathing.

We here, as yet, each day / Are blest with dear recall; as yet, alway / In some soul hold a loved continuance / Of shape and voice and glance. / But what has been will be— / First memory, then oblivion's turbid sea — Hardy

Thomas Hardy, “The To-be-forgotten”

Pipeline

Triage
A sharp articulation of one of the canon's deepest subjects: the persistence of self in the face of its erasure from another's mind, the grief of being unmade by someone whose recognition once constituted you.
The problem
The self persists but has nowhere to land — you remain unchanged while the person who knew you dissolves your existence from their side.
