Herbert spent his life analysing how authority uses endearment to bind. "My Master" is a devotional term that perfumes the mind even as it names the structure of ownership.

How sweetly doth My Master sound! My Master! / As Amber-greese leaves a rich sent / Unto the taster: / So do these words a sweet content, / An orientall fragrancie, My Master. — Herbert

George Herbert, “¶ The Odour, 2.”
