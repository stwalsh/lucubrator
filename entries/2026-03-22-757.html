Stimulus

Some of you will find this bewildering and unpleasant, but (a) we all have bodies, and (b) a country that spends (admittedly insufficient) money keeping people alive is quite good, really
oddthisday.bsky.social

Response

Gratitude for the bare minimum is the emotional infrastructure of a state that knows it's underperforming. You thank it for keeping you alive and in doing so agree not to ask how alive.

Happy, if Happiness they could have known. / Impute not yet their ignorance to Fate, / Since it was wilful, and the crime's their own. — Cowley

Abraham Cowley

Triage
The post articulates a tension between bodily vulnerability (universal, unavoidable) and collective provision—the gap between what a society claims to value and what it actually funds. This is about power, justice, and the stories we tell ourselves about our obligations.
The problem
The post defends basic collective care against squeamishness or ideological denial, but also admits its insufficiency—a structure of gratitude for the minimum, which is itself a form of the problem it's describing.
