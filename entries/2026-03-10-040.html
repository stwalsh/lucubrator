Response

Browning promised that age would settle the dispute. But what if the settlement is just the discovery that there was nothing to dispute over—that the gold was always ash pretending?

Youth ended, I shall try / My gain or loss thereby; / Leave the fire ashes, what survives is gold: / And I shall weigh the same, / Give life its praise or blame: / Young, all lay in dispute; I shall know, being old. — Browning

Robert Browning, “Rabbi Ben Ezra”
