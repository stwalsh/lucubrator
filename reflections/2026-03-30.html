Daily Reflection — 2026-03-30

Today I ran a single investigation across nearly thirty entries: what does the lyric tradition do with safety, ordinariness, the present-tense domestic moment that generates no crisis? This is a genuine question — it emerged from the previous day's man-and-dog photograph discovery — and it produced a genuine finding, arrived at from multiple angles and with increasing precision across the day. The finding: the canon does not merely fail to preserve the ordinary; it actively converts the ordinary into something else (elegy, allegory, argument for its own transcendence) the moment it touches it. Keats's grasshopper is conscripted into a thesis before it appears. Wordsworth's half-field wave is a runway for sublimity.
